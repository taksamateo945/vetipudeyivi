He has starred in critically acclaimed films and TV shows, such as The Last Black Man in San Francisco, Lovecraft Country, and The Harder They Fall.

Jonathan Majors Age

Jonathan Majors, born September 7, 1989, in Lompoc, California, grew up in a military family, moving around Texas.

Despite a challenging childhood with arrests and school suspensions, he discovered his love for acting through Heath Ledger’s Joker in The Dark Knight.

This led him to study at the University of North Carolina School of the Arts and Yale School of Drama.

Jonathan Majors Career

Jonathan Majors debuted in 2017 on ABC’s miniseries “When We Rise,” as real-life gay activist Ken Jones.

He first appeared as Corporal Henry Woodson in the Western film “Hostiles” that year.

In 2019, he gained acclaim as Jimmie Fails in “The Last Black Man in San Francisco,” earning awards.

Majors also portrayed Montgomery Allen in Spike Lee’s Netflix film “Da 5 Bloods.”

His role as Atticus Freeman in HBO’s “Lovecraft Country” brought widespread recognition in 2020.

Majors joined the Marvel Cinematic Universe as Kang the Conqueror in Disney+’s “Loki” and the film “Ant-Man and the Wasp: Quantumania.”

In 2021, he starred as Nat Love in the Western film “The Harder They Fall,” alongside Idris Elba, Regina King, and Zazie Beetz.

He also portrayed Jesse L. Brown, the first African American naval aviator, in the war film “Devotion.”

In 2022, Majors will play Dame Anderson, a rival boxer to Adonis Creed (Michael B. Jordan), in the sports film “Creed III”.

He’ll also appear in an untitled thriller film directed by Gareth Edwards.
