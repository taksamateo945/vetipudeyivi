Pee-Wee Herman faithfully recreates the dinner from A Charlie Brown Thanksgiving special

Publish date: 2024-08-11

While millions of families around America were sharing turkey dinners with their families, Pee-Wee Herman instead paid homage to a holiday classic.

The 69-year-old comedian (real name Paul Reubens) shared a photo of his dinner on Twitter, a faithful recreation of the unconventional dinner in the 1973 special A Charlie Brown Thanksgiving.

The tweet got nearly 600 retweets and over 6,200 likes in just a few hours.

‘Dinner’s ready!!! Thanks, Snoopy!!!’ the comedian said in his tweet, sent on Thursday afternoon.

The accompanying photo featured all of the items from the makeshift dinner Charlie Brown, Snoopy and Linus prepares for their friends: toast, popcorn, pretzels, jelly beans and an ice cream sundae with a cherry on top.

The original 25-minute special aired on the CBS network in 1973, and became an annual staple, though the network skipped the special in 1982, 1983 and 1988.

The special later moved to Nickelodeon and the Disney Channel in the 1990s, before ultimately shifting to ABC, along with the other Peanuts holiday specials, 1965’s A Charlie Brown Christmas, and 1966’s It’s the Great Pumpkin, Charlie Brown.

ABC aired all three specials on or near Halloween, Thanksgiving and Christmas every year from 2001 to 2019.

The specials all continued to be regular ratings hits, even decades after they originally aired.

The 2019  airing of A Charlie Brown Thanksgiving won its 8 PM timeslot with a 1.2 rating and a 6 share, with 5.52 million viewers tuning in. 

However, in 2020, all three specials – along with the entire Peanuts library – shifted to Apple TV Plus, though there is a three-day window where all of the specials are available to watch for free without a subscription.

They also struck a deal with PBS and PBS Kids to air the specials on the public television network just before the applicable holidays.

That lead to his hit TV series Pee-Wee’s Playhouse, which ran from 1986 to 1991, along with the 1988 movie Big-Top Pee-Wee.

Reubens resurrected the character with the 2016 Netflix movie Pee-Wee’s Big Holiday.
