Upload Season 2 Amazon Prime Release date, New Cast, Plot and Other Details

Publish date: 2024-03-27

The season 1 finale is included in the upload spoilers.

Season 2 of Upload is on the way, and for good cause. In 2020, Greg Daniels, who is best known for shows like The Office US and Space Force, released a brand-new comedy that many incorrectly viewed as a copy of The Good Place.

It is hardly unexpected that Upload proved to be its own beast given that Daniels hasn’t seen Michael Schur’s fantasy comedy.

He admitted to Collider, “I believe I had dinner with Mike and his wife in 2016.” “I had just finished this first draught. In 2015, I had sold it to HBO after presenting it to everyone in town. I informed Mike, “I’m submitting something tomorrow.” He also added, “I’m turning something in tomorrow.” In the end, it was The Good Place. Our creative minds were conjuring up the same ideas because we had collaborated for so long.

“I haven’t actually seen The Good Place. I didn’t want to be influenced by it when I realised that we were working in the same zone.

We were eager to learn if there will be a second season of Upload because season one ended in suspense.

We’re thankfully only a few weeks away from visiting the futuristic Lakeview afterlife and learning about the most recent events involving Nathan, Nora, and Ingrid.

Upload season 2 release date: When will it air on Amazon Prime?

On March 11, 2022, the complete second season of Upload will be available on Amazon Prime Video.

Given that the show has been in development for several years and has experienced significant delays due, you guessed it, to the pandemic, this is actually quite good timing.

“I am pleased to continue a terrific partnership with Amazon Studios and this brilliant cast and find out what happens next to Nora, Nathan, and Ingrid and their 2033 universe,” Greg Daniels said in a statement announcing the second season back in May 2020.

Season two will also be a “smart, cinematic comedy crackling with intrigue,” according to Jennifer Salke, head of Amazon Studios. She also said: “We know our global fans want to see the next chapter with Nathan and Nora, so we are greenlighting season two and are thrilled that Greg’s passion project has found such a devoted audience.”


Season two was described by Nathan Brown’s actor Robbie Amell as “having been simmering in his thoughts for a very long time.” And I believe that everybody who appreciated the first season will recognise that it will continue to develop.

The final day of filming was April 20, 2021, and the actor who plays Nathan, Robbie Amell, posted a picture with his co-stars.

Upload season 2 plot: What will happen?

Nathan is at a turning point in his (after) life, according to the official description for Season 2 of Upload. Although his ex-girlfriend Ingrid has unexpectedly arrived in Lakeview in an effort to improve their relationship, Nora, his customer service angel, still holds a special place in his heart.

Meanwhile, Nora is associated with the anti-tech rebel organisation ‘The Ludds’ and is off the radar. Season two is jam-packed with brand-new ideas from the not-too-distant future, such as Prototykes, Lakeview’s newest in-app digital baby programme, and other humorous hints at the challenges and developments to come in technology.

When Nathan downgraded himself to a “2g” at the end of season one, he was kept frozen and had very little data to deal with in the afterlife. It couldn’t have come at a worse time for anyone after learning that Ingrid had uploaded and after hearing Nora’s emotional proclamation of his affections.


Amell provided some insight into season two to ScreenRant, and it sounds like he’s particularly excited to tackle some serious scenes: “Nathan and Ingrid are back to square one, but there is still a lot to learn about the dynamics between Nathan and Nora and Nathan and Ingrid at the end of season one. Just excited, really.

“I honestly believe that the dramatic side of things with Nathan and Nora can be addressed with her leaving at the end of the first season, the danger she’s in, and the relationship kind of being left on a bit of a weird note and cliffhanger,” the author said.

Season two will also need to address the following issues:

Ingrid uploaded to Lakeview in what manner (was it suicide)? How will Nathan, Nora, and Ingrid’s love triangle—or square, if we count Byron—be resolved? What is next for Nathan and Jamie’s Beyond initiative? Additionally, who exactly killed Nathan?

Regarding the season one finale, Daniels commented (per Collider), “Well, it might have been a mistake.” “I’m not sure. I didn’t want to solve the mystery that existed at the time because it was a part of the programme.

Additionally, there is a love component, and I didn’t want to end it. I wanted to keep doing those two things into a potential future season.

“I’m conscious that some would find it annoying to not have perfect closure on everything, but if you think about the end of Harry Potter’s first movie, Voldemort is still an issue,” he continued.
